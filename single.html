Trip Highlights

  • Seeing some of the highest mountains in the world illuminated with red-orange color during the sunrise at Poon Hill.
  • Crisp, clear views of Annapurna, Dhaulagiri, Machhapuchhre, Hiunchuli, and other Himalayan peaks.
  • Suitable for all ages and experience levels.
  • Brilliant, blooming rhododendron forests during the spring.
  • Experience the warm hospitality of Gurung and other ethnic groups throughout the trek.

About Trip

Nestled within the breathtaking Annapurna region of Nepal, the Ghorepani Poon Hill Trek stands as an enticing odyssey, inviting trekkers to traverse a tapestry of diverse landscapes, enchanting villages, and awe-inspiring mountain panoramas. This trek, celebrated for its accessibility and moderate difficulty, extends a compelling invitation to both novice adventurers and seasoned mountaineers alike. As trekkers embark on this remarkable journey, they are met with an enriching experience, seamlessly blending the untamed beauty of nature with the warmth of cultural immersion.

Immersing oneself in the Ghorepani Poon Hill Trek is not merely a physical expedition; it is an exploration of the senses and a communion with the spirit of the Himalayas. Each step forward reveals a new chapter of the adventure, where the air is filled with the sweet scent of rhododendron blossoms and the melodious echoes of traditional Nepali tunes resonate through charming Gurung and Magar villages. The trail, winding through dense forests and meandering along ancient pathways, sets the stage for an unforgettable journey that transcends the ordinary, offering trekkers an escape into a realm where nature and culture harmoniously converge.

Why Ghorepani Poon Hill Trek?

The Ghorepani Poon Hill Trek is a popular trekking destination in the Annapurna region of Nepal, known for its breathtaking views, diverse landscapes, and cultural encounters. Travelers are drawn to this trek for its relatively moderate difficulty level, making it accessible to a wide range of trekking enthusiasts. The trek offers a perfect blend of natural beauty, traditional villages, and panoramic mountain vistas.

Scenic Beauty and Panoramic Views: One of the primary attractions of the Ghorepani Poon Hill Trek is the opportunity to witness sunrise views over the Annapurna and Dhaulagiri mountain ranges from the iconic Poon Hill viewpoint. The trek takes you through lush rhododendron forests, charming Gurung villages, and terraced fields, providing a picturesque backdrop throughout the journey.

Cultural Encounters: The trek offers a chance to immerse yourself in the local culture and hospitality of the Gurung and Magar communities. Traditional villages such as Ghorepani and Ghandruk provide a glimpse into the unique lifestyle, architecture, and customs of the local people.

Best Season for Ghorepani Poon Hill Trek

The optimal time to embark on the Ghorepani Poon Hill Trek is during the pre-monsoon (spring) and post-monsoon (autumn) seasons. During spring (March to May), the rhododendron forests come to life with vibrant blooms, and the weather is mild and clear. Autumn (September to November) offers excellent visibility, with crisp, cool weather and stunning mountain views. These seasons provide the most favorable conditions for a memorable trekking experience.

The Ghorepani Poon Hill Trek is an ideal choice for trekkers seeking a relatively short yet rewarding journey through the spectacular landscapes of the Annapurna region. Whether for the captivating sunrise views, cultural experiences, or the moderate trekking challenge, this trek promises an unforgettable adventure in the heart of the Himalayas.

Detailed Itinerary:

Day 01: Arrive at Tribhuvan International airport (1345m).

A representative from Shikhar Adventure will welcome you our hotel. We will prepare you with information and gear for your trek.

Day 02: Pre-trip Meeting and Sightseeing around Kathmandu valley.

After breakfast, we’ll brief you on our upcoming adventure and then go on a sightseeing tour around Kathmandu, including several UNESCO World Heritage sites. You can spend the rest of the day freely or prepare for your upcoming trek. Overnight at the hotel.

Day 03: Drive to Pokhara (820 meters) about 6 hours.

We drive about 6-7hrs on the Prithvi Highway to the scenic lakeside city of Pokhara. The scenery along with the drive, characterized by terraced rice fields and rolling hills builds our anticipation for the trek. Pokhara is a relaxing town, surrounded by nature’s beauty. Overnight at the hotel.

Day 04: One hour drive to Nayapul and trek to Tikhedhunga (1540 meters) about 4 hours.

After driving from Pokhara to Nayapul, we stop at the checkpoint and begin our trek through the terraced hillsides. The 3-hour trek leads you to Tikhedhunga, enjoying the scenery of the Bhurungdi Khola. Overnight at guesthouse.

Day 05: Trek to Ghorepani (2860 meters) about 6 hours.

After breakfast, we ascend the steep trail for a couple of hours to Ulleri. We follow the trail through the rhododendron forest. In the spring, these blooming flowers add a whole different level of beauty to the trail. After having lunch, the path leads gradually to Ghorepani, a Magar-dominated village, located in the middle of the rhododendron forest. Overnight at guesthouse.

Day 06: Early morning Hike to Poon Hill (3210meters) and Trek to Ghandruk (1940 meters) about 8 hours.

Wake up to catch one of the most famous views in the Annapurna region. The jaw-dropping view of Annapurna, Manaslu, Dhaulagiri, Machhapuchhre, and the surrounding peaks will leave you in awe. Hiking down to Ghorepani, we have breakfast and trek on. With good weather, you can enjoy the Annapurna I view. After breakfast, trek down to Ghandruk, a continual climb down through the rhododendron forest. From Tadapani, it takes about 7/8 hours to hike down to Ghandruk, a traditional Gurung village. Overnight at guesthouse.

Day 07: Trek to Nayapul. About 6 hours.

And an hour's drive back to Pokhara. After breakfast, we begin our final day of the Annapurna mini Circuit Trek. We head down through forests, enjoying our final glimpses of the Annapurna region. We’ll check out of the Annapurna Conservation Area checkpoint and drive back to Pokhara. Overnight at the hotel.

Day 08: Drive back to Kathmandu. Overnight at hotel. (can fly back if required)

From Pokhara, we drive the 6 or 7 hours back to Kathmandu via the Prithvi Highway. Upon reaching Kathmandu, we take you to your hotel. Take a rest and spend your day freely. In the evening we will provide a farewell dinner at a typical Nepali restaurant. Overnight at the hotel.

Day 09: Transfer to the international airport for your onward destination.

Transfer to Tribhuvan Airport. Thank you for choosing Shikhar Adventure as your trekking guide! We look forward to your next adventure with us.
